Dress Code

Following are the rules regarding the dress code as used during the ETDS competitions. As with all rules, the ETDS committee reserves the right to diverge from the rules stated in the document to rule in individual cases if conflict should arise.

Clothing

It is not allowed to dance in competitive dance clothes

(e.g. tailcoat, dance suit, latin shirt for competitions or tailored dance dress)

No excessive glitter, feathers, rhinestones allowed

Not on on clothing or hair

No rhinestone accessories used in competitive dancing are allowed


Clothing must cover buttocks and breasts, also whilst dancing


Competitive clothing is not allowed. Exceptions are:

  • Waistcoats (vests)
  • Latin and Ballroom trousers
  • Button-down shirts made for dancing

Shoes

Dance shoes are obligatory


Outdoor shoes and shoes with rubber soles are strictly forbidden on the dance floor


Heel protectors must be used


Use of paraffin or oil under your shoes is forbidden

We will provide scratching boards and wet towels in case the floor is slippery. No other tools are allowed.

If you choose to ignore the above rules, you may be held accountable for any damages you have caused.
