First-aid

In case of emergency

At the start of the ETDS

You will have a briefing with one of the committee members to learn about the relevant protocols in this venue; here we will also collect your contact details such that we know how to reach you when we need you in case of emergency.

Starting your shift

If you have a shift during the day: at the beginning of your shift you go to the info desk and check-in with the manager volunteers; it is important that they know you’re there.

If you have a shift during the night and you’re asleep at the beginning of the shift: you can keep sleeping peacefully. A pylon will be placed in front of your bed to indicate that you’re the person that is on first-aid duty and can be woken up when necessary.

At the end of your shift

Say goodbye to the manager volunteers, inform them of anything that they should know and release yourself from your shift. If you’re asleep at the end of your shift: keep sleeping.

I have a question!

If you need anything or find yourself in a situation where you don’t know what to do: contact the manager volunteers.

During your shift

There is a dedicated first-aid professional for the sports facility that is around 24/7, your main task is to support this person and be the first point of care when this person is not in the tournament hall.

Your shift is a standby shift, that means that you don't need to sit at the first-aid station all the time; during your shift you can compete/dance/party, we only need you to be in the tournament hall and be available to help when needed.

We ask you to take care of yourself such that you can handle your shift in a responsible way; this means that you are (relatively) well-rested and do not drink alcohol before or during your shift. If you are on duty, or are starting a shift soon, and you feel like you cannot continue this duty in a responsible manner: let the manager volunteers know as soon as possible.

For the standby shifts in the middle of the night, it is fine if you go to sleep. If you go to sleep during your shift, please place a pylon in front of your bed to indicate that you’re the person that is on first-aid duty and can be woken up when necessary. We will remove the pylon at the end of your shift.

During the competition we have scheduled one open-class dancer and one breitensport-dancer such that always one of you will be available.
