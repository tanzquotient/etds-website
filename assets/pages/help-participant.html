Participant help

So... You cannot see the forest through the trees eh? (Pun intended) You can find all the basic information you need to know before and during the weekend right here on this page. We'll assume you have been to an ETDS (or NTDS) before. If not, check out the newcomers page.

For all questions, make sure you check with your team captain first. If they don't know, ask them to ask us. That way, we can keep all information flowing through the correct channels. If they don't respond, however, and you're panicking, then of course you can send us an email.

Before the ETDS

How do I register?
Registration goes through the team captains. If you are on this website, you will likely know who your team captain is. Payment also goes through your team captain or your association. If you don't know or don't have one, please send us an email.
My activation mail is not arriving!
First try by going to https://fall.xtds.dance/activate/new/example@example.com. Where you replace example@example.com by your own email. If this does not work, and you have checked your spam, please send us an email and we'll look into it!
Is there a packing list?
We have a packing list! You can find it here!
Can I park at the venue?
Yes, there are plenty of free parking spaces available. There will be a parking attendant to guide you to the right spot.
Can I come by train?
Yes! It's only a 15-minute walk from the train station in Seesen.
Uncommon or less common things at this ETDS you should know about!
  • You need a plate, cup and cutlery: spoon, fork, knife. (But not a bowl)
  • There are no blind dating rules this ETDS. This means you can even dance with your own dance partner.
  • All latin tournaments will be held on Saturday and all standard tournaments on sunday.
  • There will not be an Open class.
  • There will be two qualification rounds.
  • Sleeping halls do not need to be emptied during the day.
  • Dinner will be served on Saturday as well.
  • Shoes are not allowed in the sleeping halls.

At the ETDS - General

So food is included? What about drinks? And what about dietary restrictions?
The food that will be provided is:
  • A snack on Friday
  • Breakfast, lunch and dinner on Saturday
  • Breakfast, lunch and dinner on Sunday
  • Breakfast on Monday

The exact menu for lunch and dinner will be on our website at least around one week before the start of the ETDS. During Breakfast and Lunch drinks like milk, coffee and tea will be provided.

There will be food provided for people with allergies, if our caterer cna handle this. There is also special food for vegetarians and vegans. This has to be mentioned during registration! People who not mentioned to be vegan/vegetarian are not allowed to take this food. You need to bring your own cutlery and plate!

You can buy extra drinks at the tournament hall. For more information on this see the food and drinks page.
If I want drinks, do I pay by card or by cash?
Drinks bought at the bar will have to be paid by card. Contactless payments are also possible. Most cards and credit cards are accepted.
Can we order food to the venue?
Yes, that should be perfectly fine
Is there mandatory volunteering?
That is correct. Every participant is expected to help out to make the ETDS happen. Only in this way can we keep the costs low. Every team is assigned a number of OHHs (Obligatory Helping Hours). Division of these goes through the team captain.

Want to help out more? Great! You can enter this in the portal after logging in. If this doesn't work, or you don't know what to do, contact your team captain. Helping extra gives your team points to win the prize for the best overall team! For more information, see the volunteering page.
Will the sleeping halls stay open during the day/have to be cleared?
Good news, the sleeping halls do not have to be cleared during the day. You can just keep all your stuff in place.
Can I charge my phone at the venue?
There will not be a designated phone charging station. This means you have to bring your own extension cords for in the sleeping halls.
What does the team presentation entail?
Generally in an ETDS, teams are just called by city and the entire team walks on the floor.
Can my friends/family come and watch?
They are very welcome to come and watch you for the competitions. Note however that registration is required and the number if tickets available is limited. For more information see the visitor information page.

Make sure to tell them the timetable, so they know when to come.

At the ETDS - Dancing

Is there a preliminary schedule?
Yep! You can find it in the schedule on the front page. It is still very subject to change.
Are there clothing restrictions?
Yep! They are generally the same as any ETDS. Check all restrictions on the dress code page!
What are the classes and in which one should I dance?
Usually during an ETDS there are different classes. However, this time, there will only be one initial class. We will all be dancing against one another, regardless of level. Then based on how you perform in two qualification rounds, you will be placed in one of 8 sub-classes:
  • Enthusiasts
  • Amateurs
  • Professionals (Profis)
  • Masters
  • Champions
  • Legends
  • Hellraisers
  • Devils


For more information, see the classes page.
Will there be a redance?
No, there will not be a redance. Since there will be two qualification rounds, you will already have 3 chances to show your dancing.
Which dances do I have to dance?
The dances that you have to dance differ per class in which you are placed. Furthermore, this may mean you get additional dances in later rounds. You can find this information here.
Are there blind date restrictions?
There are absolutely no rules regarding blind dating. This means you can dance with anyone. Even from your own team! Even your own partner!
