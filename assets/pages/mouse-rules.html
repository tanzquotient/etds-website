General rules of the Tanzmaus competition

  • Every participating city is automatically registered as a team.
  • The organizing team is not considered in the Tanzmaus competition.
  • To be considered for the Tanzmaus, every team must exist out of a minimum of 10 breitensport starts.
  • One start means one person participating in one discipline. (Standard or Latin)
  • Smaller teams may be combined to form a team that adheres to previous points.
  • A combined team may not be bigger than the largest uncombined team.
  • A combined team has to be registered before midnight on Friday evening.
  • Scoring is done through the later mentioned scoring algorithm.
  • The highest scoring team wins.

Tanzmaus scoring: Tournament (max score: ±90 for 50 starts per class)

  • Every breitensport and beginner dancer receives points per start:
    • Beginners: 0
    • Amateurs: 0
    • Profis: 10
    • Masters: 20
    • Champions: 30
  • If this is the dancer's first xTDS, and they do not already dance competitions, a bonus of 10 points is awarded.
  • For each couple that a dancer has defeated within their own class, the dancer receives 1 point.
  • If the couple is a non-obligatory blind date couple, an additional 10 points are awarded to both dancers.
  • The points for all starts in a team are summed up and divided by the number of starts.

Tanzmaus scoring: Volunteering (max score: 50)

  • For each volunteering hour (not obliged helping hours) 10 points are awarded to the team with a maximum of 50 points.
  • This score is divided by the number of all team members.
  • If there are still obliged helping hours left unfilled and your team has not reached its quota, a score of -25 is used instead. No bonus points for volunteering can be achieved in this case.

Tanzmaus scoring: Team presentation (max score: 15)

  • Each team has 30 seconds for a team presentation
  • Points are awarded to the top 3:
    • 1st place: 15 points
    • 2nd place: 10 points
    • 3rd place: 5 points

Tanzmaus scoring: Algorithm

  • The scores from Tournament, Volunteering and Team presentation are summed.
  • The highest scoring team wins.

Notes:

  • Adjudication is not considered volunteering for the Tanzmaus.
  • Super-volunteers are excluded from the team in Tanzmaus calculations.
  • The hosting team is not considered for the Tanzmaus.
  • Open dancers are not considered for the tournament scoring.
